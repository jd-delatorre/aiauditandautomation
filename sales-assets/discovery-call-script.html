Discovery Call Script

Internal Document
"Hi [Name], thanks for taking the time today. I really appreciate it. Before we dive in, I'd love to learn a little more about you and [Company] — how long have you been running the business?"

Rapport questions (pick 2-3, keep it natural):

  • How did you hear about us?
  • How long have you been running the business?
  • What's been keeping you busy lately?
  • How big is the team right now?
"That's great — thanks for sharing. I'd love to learn more about how things work day-to-day at [Company] so I can understand where there might be some automation opportunities. Mind if I ask a few questions?"
Operations & Workflow
  • Walk me through a typical day at your business. What does the morning look like?
  • What tasks eat up the most time for you or your team?
  • What do you wish you could automate but haven't figured out how?
  • How many people handle administrative or repetitive work?
Communication
  • How do customers typically reach you? (phone, email, text, WhatsApp, social?)
  • What's your average response time to a new lead or inquiry?
  • Do you have after-hours coverage? What happens to messages that come in at night?
  • How much time does your team spend answering the same questions over and over?
Scheduling
  • How are appointments or meetings currently booked?
  • How do you handle reminders and follow-ups?
  • How often do no-shows happen? What's your process for handling them?
Pain Points
  • What's the one thing that frustrates you most about your current operations?
  • Where do things fall through the cracks?
  • Have you lost clients or revenue because of slow response times or missed follow-ups?
AI Familiarity
  • Have you used any AI tools? (ChatGPT, Copilot, etc.)
  • What's your impression of AI for business — excited, skeptical, overwhelmed?
  • Have you looked into automation before? What stopped you from moving forward?
  • Company size: "How many employees do you have total?"
  • Decision-making: "Are you the person who would approve a project like this, or is there someone else involved in that decision?"
  • Budget: "Have you set aside a budget for improving operations or technology this year?"
  • Timeline: "Is this something you'd want to move on soon, or more of a future consideration?"
  • Commitment: "If the audit identifies real savings, would you be open to implementing the recommendations?"
"Based on what you've shared, I think there are some real opportunities here. Let me tell you about what we do..."
"We do something called an AI Automation Audit. We spend 2-3 hours observing how your team works — either on-site or over Zoom — and interview your key people about their daily routines and pain points. Then we go away for a few days and come back with a written report that identifies 5 to 15 specific automation opportunities in your business — each one with estimated hours saved and ROI numbers, so you know exactly what you're leaving on the table."
"The investment is $500, and here's the best part — if you decide to move forward with implementation, that $500 is credited in full toward the project. So the audit is effectively free. And if you don't move forward, you still keep the report. No obligation."
Key Talking Points
  • 2-3 hours of your team's time, that's it
  • Written report with 5-15 specific opportunities
  • ROI estimates for each — real numbers, not guesses
  • $500 — credited if you proceed with implementation
  • 1-2 weeks from kickoff to final report
  • No obligation to proceed — you keep the report regardless
  • Everything runs on YOUR servers — data stays private
"$500 is expensive for an assessment"

"I understand. The way to think about it: if the audit identifies even one opportunity that saves you 5 hours a week, that's worth tens of thousands per year. Plus, the $500 is credited in full if you proceed with implementation — making the audit effectively free. It's the lowest-risk way to find out what AI can actually do for your business."

"We're not ready for AI yet"

"That's actually the best time to do the audit. It's not about implementing AI tomorrow — it's about understanding where the opportunities are so you can plan. Think of it like getting an energy audit on your building. You learn what's possible, then decide when to act. The report gives you a roadmap you can execute on your own timeline."

"We tried AI tools before and they didn't work"

"I hear that a lot. Usually it's because there was no structured assessment of where AI actually fits. Most businesses try ChatGPT for a week and give up. What we do is different — we identify the specific workflows where automation has the highest impact, then build custom solutions for those workflows. Not generic chatbots."

"Can you just tell me what to automate?"

"I wish I could, but every business is different. What works for one marketing agency doesn't necessarily work for another. The audit process — observing your actual workflows, talking to your team — is what surfaces the real opportunities. The ones that actually save meaningful time. That's the value of the audit."

"I need to think about it"

"Of course, take the time you need. A few things that might help: the audit fee is credited toward implementation, there's no obligation to proceed after, and the sooner we identify the opportunities, the sooner you stop leaving time and money on the table. I'll send you a proposal with all the details — when would be a good time to follow up?"

"We're too small for this kind of thing"

"Businesses with 10-20 people are actually where we see the biggest impact per person. You don't have a large operations team to absorb inefficiency — every hour saved directly hits your bottom line. One of our typical findings saves 20+ hours per week. For a small team, that's transformative."

If Interested (Hot Lead)
"Great — I'll send over a proposal today with all the details. If everything looks good, we can get the kickoff call on the calendar for as early as next week. Does that work?"
If Hesitant (Warm Lead)
"No problem at all. Let me send you the proposal so you can review it at your pace. I'll include everything we discussed — the process, deliverables, and pricing. Can I follow up with you on [day, 3-5 days out]?"
If Not a Fit (Cold Lead)
"I appreciate your time today, [Name]. Even if the timing isn't right, feel free to reach out if things change. I'll send you some information about what we do in case it's useful down the road."
